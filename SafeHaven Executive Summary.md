# SafeHaven Executive Summary

## The Problem

### Domestic Violence is an Epidemic
- **1 in 4 women** experience severe intimate partner violence
- **1 in 7 men** experience severe intimate partner violence  
- **54% of trans women** experience intimate partner violence
- **Average 7 attempts to leave** before successfully escaping
- **70% cite economic dependence** as the barrier to leaving

### Most Marginalized Face Highest Risk
- **Black women**: 2.5x higher IPV rate than white women
- **Indigenous women**: 1.5x higher IPV rate, often in rural areas with no services
- **Trans women**: Highest IPV rates, fewest resources, outing used as weapon
- **Undocumented immigrants**: Fear deportation if they report abuse
- **Male survivors**: Face stigma, no shelters, often not believed by police
- **Disabled survivors**: 2x higher IPV rate, abusers weaponize disability/mobility
- **Neurodivergent survivors**: 3x higher risk (ADHD, autism, intellectual disabilities)

### Existing Solutions Fail Marginalized Survivors
- **Generic DV apps**: Assume white, cisgender, heterosexual woman with neurotypical brain
- **Shelters**: Often full, exclude trans women, no male shelters, not accessible to disabled
- **Legal aid**: Overwhelmed, long waitlists, may not understand LGBTQ/immigrant issues
- **Hotlines**: Not always culturally competent, may not have interpreters
- **Technology**: Assumes smartphone access, tech literacy, privacy (abuser monitors phone)

## The Solution: NeuroThrive SafeHaven

### What We Built
A **free, intersectional, trauma-informed** safety planning platform with:

1. **Silent Documentation System**
   - No camera sounds (stealth mode)
   - GPS off by default (safety)
   - Encrypted evidence vault
   - Court-ready PDF export

2. **Verified Document Copies**
   - SHA-256 cryptographic hashing
   - Blockchain timestamping
   - Legal-grade digital copies of birth certificates, passports, etc.
   - Solves: "I can't take the original documents without abuser noticing"

3. **Economic Empowerment**
   - Integrated job search (NeuroThrive Career)
   - Secret savings tracker
   - Budget tools
   - Skills training (free Salesforce, data entry, customer service courses)

4. **Intersectional Resource Database**
   - 1,000+ BIPOC/LGBTQIA+/male-specific resources
   - Culturally-specific services (Black-led orgs, Latina shelters, Indigenous support)
   - Immigration support (U-Visa, VAWA, no ICE contact)
   - Disability accessibility info
   - 20+ languages

5. **Panic Features**
   - Shake phone 3x → Delete all SafeHaven data
   - Dual password (real data vs. decoy content)
   - SOS button → GPS + "HELP" to emergency contacts

### Who We Serve (Priority Order)

**Most Marginalized First**:
1. Trans BIPOC women (highest risk, fewest resources)
2. Undocumented immigrants (fear deportation)
3. Disabled survivors (abusers weaponize disability)
4. Male survivors (stigma, no services)
5. LGBTQIA+ survivors (outing used as weapon)
6. Neurodivergent survivors (executive dysfunction during crisis)
7. ALL survivors (universal design benefits everyone)

### Design Principles

**1. Intersectionality is Core, Not Optional**
- Built WITH marginalized survivors, not FOR them
- BIPOC and LGBTQIA+ resources prioritized in algorithm (higher scores)
- Gender-neutral language throughout ("survivor" not "victim", "they" not "she")
- Cultural competence (faith-based options, immigration resources, tribal resources)

**2. Survivors Control Their Data**
- Zero-knowledge encryption (we can't access your data, even if subpoenaed)
- No tracking, no analytics (can't be used against you)
- Panic delete (your safety > evidence preservation)

**3. Economic Independence = Freedom**
- Job search integrated (can't leave without income)
- Target: Remote, flexible jobs (safer than in-office)
- Skills training (free certifications)
- Financial literacy (budgeting, credit building)

**4. Trauma-Informed Design**
- No graphic imagery (re-traumatizing)
- Gentle language ("you deserve safety" not "you're a victim")
- User pacing (save progress, return anytime)
- Celebrate small wins (build confidence)

**5. Accessible to ALL**
- Works on old phones (many survivors have limited tech)
- Low-literacy mode (visuals, simple language)
- Screen reader compatible (blind survivors)
- Multiple languages (Spanish, Mandarin, Arabic, ASL resources)
- Low data usage (limited phone plans)
